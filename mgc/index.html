Hoffberger Class of 2015 Show On View in New York
Steven Harvey Fine Art Projects presents a group exhibition of paintings by Henry Taylor, Erin High, Lauren Jefferson, Minsol Kim, Stephen Alexander Clark, Thomas Dahlberg, Jinie Park and Michael Evans. The exhibition includes paintings made while the group of artists were in graduate school together in Maryland Institute College of Art’s Leroy E. Hoffberger School of Painting.

Also on view is a painting by Henry Taylor, visiting artist and mentor to students at the Hoffberger School. While this group of painters diverges in approach, they are united through an investigation into painting’s continued resilience and temporality. Representation, abstraction and hybridity hold equal weight in these varied mediations whose hermetic journey from pedagogical space to gallery space culminates with this exhibition.

Curatorial Practice Alum Curated Show at Arlington Arts Center

Join us in the Wyatt Resident Artists Gallery on the Upper Level for the opening reception of 'Yes, and' - a group show curated by Caitlin Tucker-Melvin (Curatorial Practice, '14) featuring the work of the Arlington Arts Center Residents: Michele Colburn, Roxana Geffen, Hedieh Javanshir Ilchi, Becca Kallem, Bridget Sue Lambert, Pam Rogers, Rachel Schmidt, Austin Shull, Jessica van Brakle, Alice Whealin, Dawn Whitmore.

﻿Update You MICARD Sticker before September 3﻿

Validate Your MICARD
All returning Graduate students must obtain an updated sticker to validate your MICARD in order to maintain access to campus facilities. Pick up your new Fall 2015 sticker at Campus Safety Security Desks located at Lazarus Center, the Fox Building, or Brown Center before 5:00pm Thursday, September 3rd.

MICARD Solutions Center
At the beginning of the Fall semester, all staff and equipment necessary for creating a new MICARD or adding services to your existing MICARD will be temporarily relocated to Bunting Center room 207.

If you have lost your MICARD, are experiencing problems with your card, or would like to add MICA Flex Dollars or a Meal Plan, visit the MICARD Solutions Center! Please note that students who have lost their cards are subject to a Lost Card Fee of $25. Please review the MICARD Solutions Center schedule:
  • Saturday, 8/29  & Sunday, 8/30: 10:00 am - 12:00 pm [noon] (Bunting 207)
  • Monday, 8/31 & Tuesday, 9/1: 9:00 am - 4:00 pm (Bunting 207)
  • Wednesday, 9/2: MICARD resources will return to their normal locations at Campus Safety (1212 Mt. Royal Avenue) and Student Account Services (Bunting 2ndFloor) respectively.

As of 5:00 pm Thursday, September 3, 2015, all students must have an active MICARD, or you will not be able to use any meal plans or swipe into campus buildings.

Thursday, August 6 // "Phase" Opening Reception

Opening Reception
Thursday, August 6, 7-9pm
Lazarus Center, 1st Floor
Join members of GradEx for the opening reception of Phase, located in the new exhibition space next to the Graduate Office on the first floor of Lazarus Center.

Phase is a visual discussion about temporality, specifically past, present and future. The art work deals with confrontation of one’s self and childhood. Each artist's works represents a different part of time, past, present, and future These works play with ideas of linear and cyclical time.

Artists:
Henry Becker, Graphic Design, '16
Morgan Everhart, LeRoy E. Hoffberger School of Painting, '16

The Feminist Art Project Baltimore Curator-in-Residence

The Feminist Art Project Baltimore (TFAPB) is seeking a qualified and creative Curator for our new Curator-In-Residence program! CIR’s must be willing to serve a term of one year in the Baltimore City area. A project budget will be supplied, as well as a modest honorarium.

This call is open to Baltimore-area, self-identified women artists/curators. Women of color are highly encouraged to apply. CIR’s will be chosen by a diverse committee of professionals in the area. Please review the expectations and requirements for this position. The complete call and expectations can be accessed on our website at this link: http://www.thefeministartproject-baltimore.org/p/call-for-artists.html

Grad Lab Important Dates

As the summer session draws to a close we wanted to remind you of the following important dates:

Sunday August 2nd will be the last day that you can settle any outstanding Grad Lab fines from the summer session at the Grad Lab checkout desk.

Monday August 3rd we will be be turning all unpaid fines over to to Student Accounts. Once a fine has been turned over to Student Accounts, you will need to contact them to arrange for payment. Grad Lab fines are turned over to Student Accounts at the end of every semester. This applies to all students, regardless of which program you are enrolled in.

Monday August 3rd all Grad Lab computers will have their contents erased as part of our end of semester maintenance.

Call for Applications // Richard Diebenkorn Teaching Fellowship

San Francisco Art Institute and Headlands Center for the Arts are currently accepting applications for the fall 2016 Richard Diebenkorn Teaching Fellowship. The Richard Diebenkorn Teaching Fellow will receive a 25,000 USD stipend to teach two courses in the fall 2016 semester and present a public lecture in the Visiting Artists and Scholars Lecture Series. Applicants should be early to mid-career artists with an established body of work and exemplary exhibition record in the media of painting and drawing. Read the full call and apply here.

Wednesday, August 5 // Funderdome: The Art of the Pitch

Admission: $10 PURCHASE TICKETS
CUE Art Foundation
Wednesday, August 5, 2015 from 6:30 PM to 8:30 PM (EDT)
New York, NY

With grant season looming, CUE hosts a series of events designed for artists in the midst of applying for project funding. Grant writing panel, The Art of the Pitch aims to give artists an insider’s perspective of the grant review process with expert advice from professionals who work with foundations and arts councils. Our guests will share tips on how to craft a compelling proposal, and we’ll cover the top “do’s and don’ts” of applying for funding.
