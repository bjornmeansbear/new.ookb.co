Adding your new google account to your Mac/iPhone

Mac OSX Mail

Open MacOSX Mail. Find "Mail" in the upper menu and then go to preferences.

Once preferences open, click on the accounts tab.

Click on the [+] underneath listed emails/accounts

Next, select "Google" from the menu of options that pops up. Click Continue.

Enter you @rogersandgoffigon email address. Click Next.

Then enter your new password you've set. Click Next.

Select what you'd like to keep track of. Then click "done".

That should do it.

iOs Mail (iPhone and iPad)

On your iPhone or iPad you can add a new email address under settings and then finding Mail, Contacts, Calendars. Click on add account. You'll be given a list of options, select "google" from the menu of options that pops up. Enter you @rogersandgoffigon email address and then your new password you've set. Select what you'd like to keep track of. That should be all.

Find "Settings" on your iPhone or iPad.

Open settings and then scroll down until you find "Mail, Contacts, Calendars." Click on it.

This will show you the active accounts that Mail checks on your iPhone or iPad. Click on Add Account.

You will be presented with a list of presets. Select "Google" from the menu of options that pops up.

This will take you to a google login screen. Enter you @rogersandgoffigon email address. Click Next.

Then enter your new password you've set. Click Next.

Select what you'd like to keep track of. Then click "Save" in the upper right corner.

That should do it. If you'd like to give your newly added account a special name (it typically defaults to google) then click on it.

That takes you to a screen where you can change what items your account is syncing with your phone. It also shows you the account email proper. Click on your email.

That takes you to the details for the account. If you click on the text next to "description" you can give this account a custom name that is more descriptive than "gmail" — maybe RandG Gmail or Rogers and Goffigon (google) … or similar.
