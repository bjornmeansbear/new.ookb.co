Kristian Bjørnard

Educator / Sustainabilitist / Open-Sourcerer

Producer of visuals; professor of design; pedaler of bikes.

Kristian Bjørnard resides in Baltimore with his wife and child in an old stone house. He operates OOKB, a design practice focusing on books, identities, and digital tools. His work investigates spaces where design and sustainability overlap, analyzes F/LOS practices, seeks slowness, and explores making content and visual styling medium agnostic. Kristian has made biodiesel, sold kitchen-ware, grown vegetables, and collaborated with museums, artists, human resource staffing agencies, restaurants, universities, real estate developers, NGOs, and organic farmers. Kristian holds an MFA in Graphic Design from MICA and a BA in Studio Art from Kalamazoo College.
